Civil rights act of 1964

The Civil Rights Act of 1964 is a landmark civil rights and labor law in the United States that outlaws discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, and national origin.

Title I

Title I requires voting registration to be applied equally. It disallowed voters from being rejected for non material errors on the application

Title II

Title II allows lawsuit for discrimination

Title III

Title III addresses segregation and discrimination in public spaces

Title IV

Equal opportunity in higher education for minorities

Title V

No person can be denied or discriminated in finantial assistance for race, color, or national origin.
